Kabaddi is a fast-paced team sport that originated in ancient India and is now popular across South Asia. It involves two teams taking turns to send a "raider" into the opponent's half to tag players and return without being tackled. The raider must chant "kabaddi" continuously while in enemy territory, adding a unique vocal challenge to the game. Strength, strategy, and agility are key to both attacking and defending. Kabaddi has gained international recognition, with leagues and tournaments held worldwide. The apple is one of the most popular and widely consumed fruits around the world. Known for its crisp texture and sweet-tart flavor, apples come in a variety of colors including red, green, and yellow. Rich in fiber, vitamins, and antioxidants, they are not only delicious but also offer numerous health benefits. Apples are often eaten fresh, but they can also be baked into pies, made into sauces, or pressed into juice. With their symbolic presence in folklore, mythology, and even science—think Newton’s apple—they hold a special place in both culture and cuisine.

Banana

The banana is a soft, sweet fruit that grows in tropical regions and is loved for its convenience and nutritional value. Wrapped in a peel that’s easy to remove, bananas are a popular on-the-go snack. They are rich in potassium, vitamin B6, and dietary fiber, making them great for heart health and digestion. Bananas can be eaten fresh, blended into smoothies, baked into breads, or sliced over cereal. Their natural sugars provide a quick energy boost, and their creamy texture makes them a favorite in both sweet and savory dishes.
