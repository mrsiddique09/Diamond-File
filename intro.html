A prominent Islamic Instiution in Karnataka, Indi

Jamia Islamia Bhatkal is a prominent Islamic seminary in Karnataka, known for its scholarly heritage and contributions to religious education. Established in 1962, Jamia Islamia Bhatkal is located in the coastal town of Bhatkal and affiliated with Darul Uloom Nadwatul Ulama, Lucknow. The seminary was founded to provide Islamic education to the local Muslim community, with its roots tracing back to early efforts in the 1920s and 1950s by scholars like Dr. Ali Malpa and Mohammed Ismail Akrami.

The campus spans over 20 acres in Jamia Abad and includes a UGC-certified research-level library housing over 40,000 books in Arabic and English. Jamia has hosted major national events, including the All India Muslim Personal Law Board conference in 2009 and the Muslim World League convention in 2012. It publishes magazines in Urdu (Armaghan-e-Hijaz) and Arabic (Az-Zahra) to promote scholarly discourse. The institution continues to play a vital role in nurturing Islamic scholarship and community leadership in the region.
